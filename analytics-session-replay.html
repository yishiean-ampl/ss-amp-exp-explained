📈 See What Moved and Why: Analytics + Session Replay

Pulse tells you a metric moved. This course covers the two questions Pulse can't answer: where in your product it happened (funnels) and why users behaved that way (Session Replay). Every concept comes with 🧒 ELI6 and a 🔵 Amplitude equivalent.

⏱ 20–25 min🧰 Unit 1 · Analytics Layer 🔻 Unit 2 · The Funnel Split🎥 Unit 3 · Session Replay 🏁 Cumulative Quiz
Course Opening

The three-tool investigation chain 🔎

1. Pulse 📊WHAT moved? Aggregate metric effect with statistical testing.
2. Funnel split 🔻WHERE in the flow? Group By experiment/gate group, find the divergence step.
3. Session Replay 🎥WHY? Watch exactly the sessions of the variant that dropped.
🧒 ELI6 — the whole course in one story Your lemonade stand sold less today (what — the scoreboard). You notice kids walk up, look at the price sign, and leave (where — the exact step they quit). So you hide behind the tree and watch: turns out the new sign is upside down! 🙃 (why — you had to SEE it).
🔵 Amplitude equivalent This is Statsig's version of a chain you already demo daily: Amplitude Experiment results → Funnel Analysis chart → jump into Session Replay from the chart of users who dropped. Same investigation, different anchor: Statsig anchors on the experiment/gate exposure; Amplitude anchors on the chart. Keep that contrast in your pocket — it's the theme of all three units.
Unit 1 · ~7 min

🧰 Your Post-Ship Analytics Layer: Metrics Explorer

After you ship a gate or conclude an experiment, Pulse gives you the aggregate result. Metrics Explorer gives you everything else: trends, funnels, retention, distributions, and dashboards that maintain themselves.

🔮 Predict first You tag all checkout gates/experiments "checkout" and create a dashboard also tagged "checkout." Next week a teammate ships a new checkout experiment without touching your dashboard. How many new charts appear?
One — automatically. Tag-based auto-populate adds a snapshot card for every new gate/experiment with a matching tag. Try it below. 👇

LO3 · Metric Drilldown: find WHICH slice contains the lift 🔬

One metric over time, sliced by user properties, event properties, time window, and source. Investigation path: time → user segment → event source → compare windows, until you find the slice that contains the entire movement.

🧒 ELI6 The cookie jar is emptying faster this week 🍪. Drilldown = checking WHEN it empties (after school!), WHICH kid (your brother!), WHERE (kitchen, not lunchbox). You keep slicing until one slice explains everything.
🔵 Amplitude equivalent This is Segmentation with group-bys and property filters — your bread and butter. The workflow (slice by time, segment, source until the anomaly isolates) is identical; Amplitude adds deeper segmentation and cohort tooling on top.

Unit 2 · ~8 min

🔻 The Funnel Split: Finding WHERE the Effect Happened

🔮 Predict first Your experiment shows a 3% drop in "checkout completed" for treatment. You build a 4-step funnel split by experiment group. The lines diverge at step 3. What does that tell you, and where do you look next?
The WHERE is isolated: treatment causes drop-off at step 3 specifically — steps 1–2 are unaffected, everything after step 3 is a consequence. Next stop: Session Replay triggered on the treatment assignment, to watch what those users hit at step 3. 👇

LO4 · Build funnels from 3–5 DECISION points, not page loads 🪜

🎮 Simulator: compounding attrition — why 7-step funnels lie to you

FINISH THE FUNNEL
41%

At 80% per step, a 7-step funnel ends at ~21% — and with 7 candidate steps you can't tell which one is "the problem." Keep it to 3–5 decision events. ✂️

🧒 ELI6 Every extra hoop loses some kids, even if each hoop is easy. Seven easy hoops in a row and most of the class never gets the prize — and you can't tell which hoop was the annoying one because they ALL leaked a little.

🚨 Gotcha The funnel split is NOT Pulse. Pulse = aggregate effect with statistical testing ("is it real?"). Funnel split = visual step attribution without per-step stats ("where is it?"). A 3% aggregate drop can show up as a 15% drop at one step. Use both, for different questions.
🧒 ELI6 Two lines of kids going through the same obstacle course. They stay side-by-side until the monkey bars — then the blue team suddenly thins out. You haven't proven WHY, but you know exactly WHERE to point the camera. 🎥
🔵 Amplitude equivalent A Funnel Analysis chart grouped by flag/experiment variant. Same divergence-hunting workflow; in Amplitude you'd then jump straight from the funnel step into Session Replay of the users who dropped — chart-anchored, versus Statsig's exposure-anchored triggers (Unit 3).

Unit 3 · ~7 min

🎥 Session Replay as Targeted Investigation

You know WHAT dropped and WHERE. Replay answers WHY — but only if it records the sessions that matter. Statsig ties recording directly to gate exposures and experiment assignments.

🔮 Predict first Your trigger records sessions when a user is assigned to treatment. A user browses for 45 seconds BEFORE being assigned. How many seconds of pre-assignment behavior do you see?
30 seconds. Statsig keeps a 30-second rolling buffer before the trigger, so you get the last 30s of context + everything after. Watch it below. 👇

LO8 · Conditional triggers + the 30-second rewind buffer ⏪

🎬 Animation: a session being captured

🧪 assigned to treatment (t=45s)
User lands on the site… browsing… (not recorded yet)
not captured 30s rewind buffer 🕰️ recording 🔴

Trigger types: ① individual gate exposure (pass or fail) · ② individual experiment exposure (specific variant) · ③ ALL gate/experiment exposures (needs SDK 3.30.1+) · ④ any specific event.

🧒 ELI6 It's a dashcam 🚗. It's always quietly remembering the last 30 seconds, but only SAVES the video when something interesting happens (the trigger). So when the "crash" happens, you also get the 30 seconds leading up to it.
🚨 Gotcha "Replay records everything — I'll drown in sessions." Nope: conditional triggers mean you record ONLY, say, treatment-variant sessions. You watch exactly the users who dropped at step 3, not the whole library.

LO9 · The Global Targeting Gate is the OUTER control 🎛️

🎮 Simulator: will this session be recorded?

🌍 Global Targeting Gate — who may EVER be recorded (exclude PII-sensitive segments, internal envs…)
🎚️ Global Sampling Rate — % of eligible sessions recorded from session start (default 100%)
🧪 Conditional trigger — start recording on this specific exposure/event
🧒 ELI6 Russian dolls 🪆. The outer doll (targeting gate) decides who's even allowed in the game. If someone's outside the outer doll, it doesn't matter how loudly the inner doll (trigger) shouts "record!" — nothing happens.
🚨 Gotcha "My trigger is set but 0 sessions recorded!" Check in order: ① is the segment excluded by the Global Targeting Gate? ② is Session Replay initialized/enabled for the project? ③ using the "all exposures" trigger? That needs SDK ≥ 3.30.1. ④ has traffic actually hit the trigger yet?
🔵 Amplitude equivalent Amplitude Session Replay = close match on capability. The difference is the anchor: Statsig SR triggers off gate/experiment exposures (experimentation-first); Amplitude SR integrates with Analytics charts — jump from a funnel straight into replays of users who dropped (analytics-first). Same destination, different front door. Great compare-contrast moment in a demo.

🏁 Cumulative Quiz — 5 questions, all units

The workflow one last time: Pulse (what?) → Funnel split by variant (where?) → Session Replay on that variant (why?). Replay is most powerful when focused — trigger on exactly the variant you care about. Aim for 4/5. 💪

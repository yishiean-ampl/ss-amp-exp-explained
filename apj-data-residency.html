🎯 The Problem

The right answer for APJ data residency is Warehouse Native. If the customer's warehouse is deployed in an APJ region, residency is resolved at the infrastructure level — that's the conversation to have first. Within Warehouse Native, three approaches cover how strictly the customer needs to control where data flows.

🧒 ELI6: Some countries have a rule: the toys must stay in the house. Statsig's clubhouse is in America — so the game is deciding which toys (if any) ever visit the clubhouse, and which never leave your house at all. Warehouse Native means the games are played in YOUR house; these three paths decide what happens to the scorecards.

1️⃣ Path 1 — Analyze Only

The customer tracks their own exposures 📝

Every flag view or experiment enrollment is recorded by the customer's own systems. Statsig never receives the raw exposure records — the customer brings data to Statsig only for analysis (the stats engine runs in their warehouse).

✅ Residency story: CLEAN — Statsig never receives raw exposures
🧒 ELI6: You keep your own scorebook at home. The referee never touches it — they just come over to your table to do the math.

Trade-off: no Statsig-managed feature flags or real-time targeting. Right for customers with mature experimentation infrastructure who want best-in-class stats on top.

2️⃣ Path 2 — Statsig Private Data Store (Hashed, Per-Customer)

SDK logs exposures → hashed, isolated, then purged 🔐

The SDK sends exposure records to Statsig as usual, but they're fully hashed and logically isolated per customer. After 30 days (configurable, reducible), raw records are purged — only aggregations remain.

30 days of hashed raw exposure records, isolated in a per-customer store.
⚠️ Residency story: still touches US infrastructure — hashed + isolated
🧒 ELI6: Your notes get scrambled into secret code, locked in a box with only YOUR name on it at the clubhouse — and after 30 days the box goes through the shredder. Only the tally on the whiteboard survives.

Best for: data commingling / privacy concerns. Not a substitute for Paths 1 or 3 when there's a hard legal requirement that data never leaves the country.

3️⃣ Path 3 — SDK Logs Exposures to the Customer's Warehouse

Full Statsig flags, zero exposure data at Statsig 🚚

The SDK stays active for flag delivery, but exposures route to the customer's own warehouse. Statsig is never in the exposure data path. Two implementations:

Option A — Proxy + Queue (available today): customer stands up a simple proxy/API server → message queue (Pub/Sub on GCP, Kinesis on AWS) → warehouse. Manual to set up, straightforward to build.

Option B — SDK Provider Override (in prioritization queue): a callback/provider hook at the SDK level — similar to what Amplitude Experiment supports — routing exposures to Segment (or another CDP), which forwards to the warehouse. No custom proxy required.

✅ Residency story: CLEAN — exposures go straight to the customer's warehouse
🧒 ELI6: A mail slot that drops straight into your own vault. Statsig still delivers the game rules to your door (flags) — but it never carries your letters back to the clubhouse.

Trade-off: Option A = customer builds and runs the proxy + queue. Option B = wait for Statsig to ship the SDK feature.

🛡️ Objection Handling

"We can't have user data leaving the region."
Depends how strict the requirement is. A hard legal block on any US transfer → Analyze Only (customer tracks exposures; Statsig never receives them) or the SDK proxy (all data stays on customer infrastructure). If the concern is really privacy or isolation, the private data store handles it: hashed exposures in a dedicated per-customer bucket.
"You said Statsig stores raw exposure data. Where, and for how long?"
When the SDK is active, exposures go to Statsig's regional receiving servers (data isn't logged across the Pacific). Retention is 30 days by default, reducible — after that, raw records are purged and only aggregated results remain. If any retention at Statsig is unacceptable → Path 1 or Path 3.
"We're on BigQuery. Does Warehouse Native solve our residency?"
No. BigQuery is US-based — Warehouse Native runs the stats engine inside the warehouse, but a US warehouse breaks the residency story. Snowflake or Databricks deployed in an APJ cloud region is the WHN path that works.
"What data is sent to Statsig from the SDK?"
Evaluation IDs, browser/device metadata, and any user properties sent in the flag evaluation — the latter could include PII. This data is only visible in exposure streams.
